## What "Rent a Proxy" Actually Means (Quick Definition)

Renting a proxy means paying a provider for temporary access to one or more IP addresses that route your traffic through their servers. Instead of using your home or office IP for every request, your traffic appears to come from the rented IP. You pay monthly (sometimes pay-as-you-go), and when you cancel, the IPs go back to the pool.

That's it. No softwareownership, no infrastructure to manage, no datacenter contracts. You're essentially leasing identity layers on the internet.

## FAQ: What People Actually Ask Before Renting a Proxy

**Q: Is it legal to rent a proxy?**
A: Yes, in most countries. Renting and using a proxy is legal. What you do with it determines whether you cross any lines — scraping public data is generally fine, scraping data behind authentication wals or violating a site's terms of service is a different question. Stick to public data and you're operating in well-established territory.

**Q: How many proxies do I need to rent?**
A: Depends entirely on your request volume. For light scraping (under 10,000 requests/day), 10-25 datacenter proxies are plenty. For heavy automation (100k+ requests/day), look at 100-500 proxies and consider rotating residential to reduce ban risk. Start small, scale based on actual block rates.

**Q: What's the difference between datacenter and residential proxies?**
A: Datacenter proxies come from servers in commercial data centers — fast, cheap, but easier for target sites to identify as proxy traffic. Residential proxies come from real home internet connections — slower and more expensive, but they look like organic users. Pick based on how aggressively your target site filters proxy traffic.

**Q: Can I cancel anytime?**
A: Yes. Webshare runs on monthly billing for most plans (annual options are available at discount). You can cancel through the dashboard without caling anyone or filing out a form. If you cancel mid-month, your proxies stay active until the cycle ends.

**Q: Do I need technical skills to use rented proxies?**
A: For basic use, no. Webshare's dashboard hands you a list of proxies in copy-paste format that you plug into your browser, scraping tool, or app. For advanced rotation logic and integration with frameworks like Scrapy or Playwright, basic developer skills help. There are documentation pages and Discord support if you get stuck.

**Q: What happens if a rented proxy gets blocked?**
A: Webshare automatically rotates damaged IPs out of the pool. For dedicated and static plans, support will replace flagged IPs on request. For shared and residential rotating, the pool itself rotates so individual blocks don't take you offline.
